A pocket file on Gaskins

Stuff to send the friend who just asked. The case at a glance, a few lines you can quote, and the poster in every shape you actually need.

By Marlowe Hatcher

Three different people texted me about this movie last week. None of them had read a word about the case. All of them wanted to "post something." This is what I've been sending them — a small kit they can crib from, and the poster sized so it doesn't look like a thumb wrestled it.

Use whatever's useful. If you do post, please skip the killer-fan-cam edits. The women he murdered are still mostly unnamed in the public record; they don't need to share a slideshow with him.

One ask, if you post

The thing this case keeps teaching me — and the thing I hope the film holds onto — is that the people he killed mattered, and most of them never got the institutional attention their cases deserved. If you're going to put the poster on your story, consider also putting a sentence about that. The hook isn't the killer. The hook is what we missed.
