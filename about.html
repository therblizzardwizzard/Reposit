About

A reader's notebook, kept in public

Who's writing this thing, and why.

My name's Marlowe Hatcher. I grew up between Florence and Conway, South Carolina, in a stretch of pine country where the local stories never quite made it past the county line. I've been reading about those stories ever since.

I'm not a journalist. I don't have sources. What I have is a wall of paperbacks, a borrower's card at three regional libraries, and a stubborn interest in the cases that the bigger podcasts and documentaries haven't quite gotten right.

What I write about

Mostly: cold cases and convicted offenders in the Carolinas, Georgia, and the Gulf states. Occasionally: a case from somewhere else that I can't stop thinking about. Lately, more than I expected: the true crime stories that the film and television industry is finally waking up to.

I try to write the way I'd want to be written about if I were one of the named victims in a case file: carefully, without relish, with the dignity restored where the original reporting stripped it away.

What this isn't

It isn't sponsored. It isn't part of any production company's press push. I'm not on anyone's mailing list and I don't take press screeners. If a studio sends me something I'll say so on the page.

It also isn't a forum for celebrating the people I write about. If you're here for fan-club content about killers, you'll be bored fast.
